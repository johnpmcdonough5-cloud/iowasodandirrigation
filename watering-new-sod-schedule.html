Watering New Sod: A Week-by-Week Schedule

The first few weeks make or break your lawn — here's a simple plan to lock in deep, healthy roots.

Fresh sod isn't rooted into the soil beneath it yet, which means it dries out much faster than an established lawn. Getting the watering right in the first few weeks is the single biggest factor in how well your new lawn takes hold.

Week 1: Keep It Consistently Moist

Water once or twice a day, enough to keep the sod and the top inch of soil moist to the touch at all times. Light, frequent watering matters more than a single deep soak this week.

Week 2: Start Stretching the Intervals

Cut back to once a day, watering a little longer each session. This encourages roots to grow downward in search of moisture instead of staying near the surface.

Week 3: Every Other Day

By now the tug test should show real resistance. Move to watering every other day, with deeper soaks each time, to keep pushing root growth further down.

Week 4 and Beyond: Transition to a Normal Schedule

Once roots are established, shift to 2-3 deep waterings per week — about an inch of water total — the same routine that keeps a mature Iowa lawn healthy through summer.

Watch for These Warning Signs

Curling or graying blades mean it's drying out; spongy soil or visible pooling means it's getting too much. Adjust the schedule to the weather, not just the calendar.

The Bottom Line

Consistency beats perfection. A predictable watering routine for the first month sets your lawn up to thrive for years — and a smart irrigation system makes it effortless.
