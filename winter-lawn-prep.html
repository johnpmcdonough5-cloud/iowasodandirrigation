Getting Your Iowa Lawn Ready for Winter

A few fall steps keep your turf strong through the cold months and ready to green up fast in spring.

Eastern Iowa winters are tough on lawns, but the work you do in fall matters more than anything you can do once the snow arrives. A little prep now means a faster, greener comeback next spring.

Keep Mowing Until Growth Stops

Don't put the mower away too early. Keep cutting at your normal height until the grass stops growing, then drop to a slightly shorter final cut to reduce the risk of matting and snow mold under winter snow cover.

Fertilize for Root Strength

A fall feeding builds root reserves your lawn draws on all winter, fueling faster green-up in spring instead of it starting from scratch.

Aerate and Overseed Thin Spots

Fall is the best time to aerate compacted soil and overseed bare or thinning areas. Cooler air and warm soil give new grass the ideal window to establish before winter.

Clear Leaves and Debris

A thick layer of wet leaves left over winter blocks light and traps moisture, inviting mold and dead patches. Keep beds and grass clear right up until snowfall.

Winterize Your Irrigation System

Have your sprinkler lines blown out and shut down before the first hard freeze. Water left in the pipes can freeze, expand, and crack fittings — a costly, avoidable repair come spring.

The Bottom Line

A little fall maintenance goes a long way. Handle these steps before the cold sets in, and you'll be back to a thick, green lawn faster next season.
